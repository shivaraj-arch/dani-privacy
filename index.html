Dani — Privacy Policy

Effective date: 12 June 2026 · Applies to the Dani Android app (com.dani.translator)

Dani is a real-time voice translation app. This policy explains what data the app handles, why, and what happens to it.

Information we collect

  • Account information: your email address and display name, used to create and sign in to your account.
  • Contacts you add inside Dani: the list of other Dani users you connect with. We never read your phone's contact book.
  • Presence: whether you are online and when you were last seen, shown to your Dani contacts.
  • Language preference: your chosen listening language.

Voice audio and transcripts

  • During a call or Solo session, your microphone audio is streamed in real time to our translation service, which uses Google's Gemini API to produce the translated speech and transcripts. Audio is processed transiently to deliver the translation; we do not keep recordings of your calls on our servers after the call ends.
  • Live transcripts are displayed during the call. If you choose to download a transcript or recording, the file is saved locally on your device and is under your control.
  • Audio and text processed by Google's Gemini API are handled in accordance with Google's API terms.

Permissions

  • Microphone — required to capture your speech for translation during calls and Solo sessions. The microphone is only active during a session.

Service providers

Dani relies on the following processors to operate:

  • Supabase — authentication, account data, presence and app configuration.
  • LiveKit Cloud — real-time audio transport between call participants.
  • Google (Gemini API) — speech translation and transcription.

We do not sell your data, show ads, or share your information with third parties beyond the processors listed above.

Data retention and deletion

Account data is retained while your account is active. To delete your account and associated data, email us at the address below and we will remove it within 30 days.

Children

Dani is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.
